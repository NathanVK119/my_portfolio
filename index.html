About Me

I’m a fresh graduate with a degree in BS Information Technology. I have experience in web development as a freelancer, helping classmates and students build websites and connect them with databases. I enjoy exploring and researching different paths to find what truly fits me. I’ve tried web development, networking, even cybersecurity, and gained basic skills in each of them. Eventually, I discovered manual QA, and it caught my interest through bug bounty topics in cybersecurity. But what made me more curious was how bugs exist in the systems I develop myself.

I’ve always been detail-oriented, especially when it comes to UX design. I love checking websites, analyzing how they work, and suggesting better ways to improve user experience. That’s when I realized that I enjoy testing and analyzing more than just coding. I wanted a path where I could still apply my programming knowledge, and knowing the web development cycle really helps. Then I found QA Automation and it matched everything I was looking for: analysis, testing, system logic, and even a bit of that “pen tester mindset” from cybersecurity. I also believe in using AI as a helpful tool to make testing even better.

Out of all the paths I explored, QA Automation is the one I’m most passionate about. I’m ready to grow, keep learning continuously, be flexible, and fully adapt to the world of QA and the tech industry as a whole.
